# Safaricom Financial Metrics & Reporting Protocol

## 1. Core Financial Scale
- **Annual Revenue:** KES 300B+ (~USD 2.3B).
- **Profitability:** Benchmark against an EBITDA margin of ~50%.
- **Revenue Segments:** Mobile Services (Voice/Data), M-PESA (~35-40%), Fixed/Enterprise (10-15%).

## 2. Priority KPI Definitions
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User):** Primary driver for quality-of-growth analysis.
- **Service Revenue:** Analyze core business performance excluding handset sales.
- **Capex Ratio:** Monitor capital expenditure as a % of revenue (critical for 5G/Ethiopia tracking).
- **Churn & Active Users:** Focus specifically on 30-day active M-PESA and data users.

## 3. IFRS Reporting Standards
- **IFRS 15 (Revenue):** Protocol for bundling telecom services and M-PESA transaction recognition.
- **IFRS 16 (Leases):** Essential for assessing liabilities in network infrastructure and tower leases.
- **IFRS 9 (Financial Instruments):** Governing credit risk for M-PESA lending (Fuliza/M-Shwari) and expected credit loss (ECL) provisions.



## 4. Multi-Currency & Regional Risk
- **Exposure:** KES (Primary), USD (Settlements), ETB (Ethiopia Operations).
- **Ethiopia Metrics:** Track subscriber acquisition vs. capex deployment and the path to EBITDA breakeven (Greenfield network stage).

## 5. Capital Allocation & Shareholder Returns
- **Investment Focus:** 5G rollout, Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH), and Ethiopia Greenfield build-out.
- **Dividends:** Target an 80%+ payout ratio.
- **Obligations:** Spectrum fees, CA levies (Kenya), and ECA regulatory fees (Ethiopia).
